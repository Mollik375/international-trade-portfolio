What I Learned

Throughout this course, I learned many important concepts related to international trade, including comparative advantage, opportunity cost, the gravity model, production possibilities frontiers, and the Heckscher-Ohlin theory. These concepts helped me understand why countries trade and how trade can increase economic efficiency.

Skills Developed

By completing the projects in this portfolio, I improved my analytical thinking, problem-solving abilities, research skills, and data interpretation. I also gained experience creating visual demonstrations and presenting economic concepts through web technologies.

Personal Reflection

One of the most interesting aspects of this course was seeing how economic theories can explain real-world trade patterns. Some concepts were initially difficult to understand, especially opportunity costs and factor endowments, but working through examples and projects helped strengthen my understanding.

Overall, this course improved my understanding of global trade and how economic decisions affect countries, businesses, and consumers. The knowledge gained from this course will be valuable for future academic studies and professional development.
